In short

Tiaki is a coordinated platform for community-led predator control across Aotearoa. We collect only what we need to run your group's trap lines, records, and reports, and we do not sell or share that data with advertisers or marketers.

  • Your account details stay inside your group's context.
  • Catch and observation records belong to the group that recorded them.
  • You can request a copy or deletion of your personal data from your group's Coordinator or a platform administrator.

What we collect

Account information

  • Username, email, hashed password.
  • First and last name, phone, address, emergency contact details (optional — used by Coordinators for field-safety coordination).
  • Profile photo (optional).
  • Login activity (last login timestamp).

Group activity

  • Group memberships, role within each group, join dates.
  • Trap lines and traps you own or operate, including coordinates, status, and operator assignments.
  • Catch records: date, location, species, status, trap condition, notes, and the operator who recorded them.
  • Observation records: similar shape, plus optional photos.

Operational data

  • Server logs (IP address, user agent, request path, timing). Used for debugging and security; retained for 30 days then rotated.

How we use your data

  • Authenticating you and authorising access to your group's data.
  • Rendering dashboards, reports, and exports for your role.
  • Letting Coordinators manage their group's membership and operator assignments.
  • Reconciling catch and observation records against the national trap.nz CSV format for groups that choose to export.
  • Debugging incidents and recovering from data loss.

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing analytics, or profiling. We do not sell your data.

Who can see what

  • You can see your own profile, the records you authored, and any group context you've joined.
  • Operators in your group can see trap-line assignments and the catch / observation records relevant to lines they operate.
  • Coordinators of your group can see all membership, line, and record data for that group.
  • Super Administrators can see all groups and users on the platform, for safety and support reasons.
  • Public groups expose a landing page with group name, cover photo, and aggregate counts — never individual records or member contact details.

Your rights

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 you can ask us to:

  • Confirm what personal information we hold about you.
  • Give you a copy of that information.
  • Correct it if it's wrong.
  • Delete your account and the personal data associated with it.

For most requests, contact your group's Coordinator first. For platform-wide requests, contact a Super Administrator via the channels below.

How long we keep your data

  • Active account records — for as long as you have an active membership in at least one group.
  • Deactivated account records — retained for 12 months after your last membership ends, then deleted.
  • Catch and observation records — retained indefinitely as part of the group's conservation history; if you leave a group, your authored records stay in the group's archive but your name is anonymised on request.
  • Server logs — 30 days.

Security

  • Passwords are hashed at rest (never stored or logged in plain text).
  • Database access is gated behind application role checks; super-admin reads are intentional, not casual.
  • Cover and profile photo uploads are stored on the platform's file system; uploaded files are scoped per-group.

Contact

For privacy questions or data requests, contact a group Coordinator or a platform administrator. A dedicated privacy contact channel will land alongside the formal launch.
