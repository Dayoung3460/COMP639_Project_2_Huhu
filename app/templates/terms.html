Accepting these terms

By creating an account or joining a group on Tiaki, you agree to these terms. If you don't agree, please don't use the platform. We may update these terms; when we do, the date at the top of the page changes and we'll surface the update on your next login.

Your account

  • Keep your login credentials private. You're responsible for activity that happens through your account.
  • The personal information you enter must be accurate — Coordinators rely on it for safety coordination (emergency contact, address) and reporting.
  • You may have one account per email address. Re-using someone else's account is not permitted.
  • Tell a Coordinator or platform administrator promptly if you think your account has been accessed without your permission.

Acceptable use

Tiaki exists to support community-led predator control. Use it for that. In particular, you agree not to:

  • Submit falsified or deliberately misleading catch / observation records — conservation data is used to inform regional planning and bad data has real-world impact.
  • Upload content that you don't have the right to share (someone else's photos, personal information about people who haven't consented, copyrighted material).
  • Attempt to access groups, records, or administrative features you're not authorised to see.
  • Use the platform to harass other users.
  • Probe, scrape, or stress-test the platform without prior written permission from a platform administrator.

Data you contribute

Records you create (catches, observations, line operations) belong to the group that recorded them and form part of that group's conservation history. By contributing, you grant Tiaki a non-exclusive licence to store, display, and aggregate that data for the platform's normal operation, including export to formats compatible with national systems such as trap.nz.

You can request that your name be anonymised on records you previously authored if you leave a group — see the privacy policy for the data-rights process.

Platform intellectual property

The Tiaki name, logo, code, and user-interface design are owned by the platform's maintainers. You may reference the platform in conservation-related publications without additional permission. Forking the codebase or rebranding the platform requires written permission.

Suspension and termination

  • You can leave a group at any time through your profile.
  • You can request full account deletion — see the privacy policy.
  • A Coordinator can remove a member from their group; a platform administrator can suspend or remove an account that violates these terms.
  • Suspension or removal does not delete a group's conservation records authored before the action; those remain in the group's archive (with anonymisation on request).
